Learn About Wildfires

Causes

Wildfires can ignite from various sources:

  • Natural causes like lightning strikes are responsible for a significant number of wildfires, especially in dry regions.
  • Human activities such as unattended campfires, discarded cigarettes, and arson contribute to nearly 85% of wildfires.
  • Environmental factors like prolonged drought, high temperatures, and strong winds create ideal conditions for fire to spread rapidly.

Prevention

  • Clear dry vegetation and flammable materials around your property.
  • Follow local fire regulations, especially during high-risk seasons.
  • Never leave campfires or grills unattended, and extinguish them completely with water and dirt.
  • Report suspicious smoke or illegal burning immediately to local authorities.

Interesting Wildfire Facts

  • Wildfires can travel at speeds up to 14 miles per hour, especially with the help of strong winds.
  • Some wildfires can burn for months, even after the flames appear to be extinguished, due to underground hot spots.
  • Wildfires can create their own weather, including fire-induced thunderstorms and tornado-like fire whirls.
  • In some cases, wildfires help certain ecosystems thrive by clearing out dead vegetation, allowing new plant life to grow.
  • Wildfires release a significant amount of carbon dioxide, contributing to global warming and affecting air quality in large areas.
